FAR PART 9 — CONTRACTOR QUALIFICATIONS

9.1 Responsible Prospective Contractors

9.100 Scope of subpart.
This subpart prescribes policies and procedures for determining contractor responsibility.

9.101 Definitions.
Debarment means action taken by a debarring official under 9.406 to exclude a contractor from Government contracting and Government-approved subcontracting for a reasonable, specified period.

Suspension means action taken by a suspending official under 9.407 to disqualify a contractor temporarily from Government contracting and Government-approved subcontracting.

9.102 Policy.
(a) Purchases shall be made from, and contracts shall be awarded to, responsible prospective contractors only.
(b) No purchase or award shall be made unless the contracting officer makes an affirmative determination of responsibility.

9.103 Procedures.
(a) The contracting officer shall determine that the prospective contractor is responsible prior to awarding a contract.
(b) The contracting officer shall consider information from various sources including the System for Award Management (SAM), past performance information, and financial capability assessments.

9.104 Standards.
9.104-1 General standards.
To be determined responsible, a prospective contractor must —
(a) Have adequate financial resources to perform the contract, or the ability to obtain them;
(b) Be able to comply with the required or proposed delivery or performance schedule;
(c) Have a satisfactory performance record;
(d) Have a satisfactory record of integrity and business ethics;
(e) Have the necessary organization, experience, accounting and operational controls, and technical skills;
(f) Have the necessary production, construction, and technical equipment and facilities; and
(g) Be otherwise qualified and eligible to receive an award under applicable laws and regulations.

9.104-2 Special standards.
When it is necessary for a particular acquisition, the contracting officer shall establish special standards of responsibility. Special standards may be particularly important for acquisitions of security systems, IT services, or critical infrastructure support.

9.104-5 Subcontractor responsibility.
(a) Generally, prospective prime contractors are responsible for determining the responsibility of their prospective subcontractors.

9.105 Procedures.
9.105-1 Obtaining information.
The contracting officer shall obtain sufficient information to determine whether the prospective contractor is responsible, including:
(a) Financial capability (credit reports, financial statements);
(b) Performance history (past performance evaluations, references);
(c) Technical capability and capacity;
(d) Compliance with labor laws and equal opportunity requirements.

9.2 Qualifications Requirements

9.202 Policy.
Agencies may establish qualification requirements applicable to both products and producers, testing requirements, or other measures to ensure contractor qualification.

9.3 First Article Testing and Approval

9.301 Definition.
First article means a preproduction model, initial production sample, test sample, first lot, pilot lot, or pilot model.

9.4 Debarment, Suspension, and Ineligibility

9.400 Scope of subpart.
This subpart prescribes policies and procedures governing the debarment and suspension of contractors.

9.401 Applicability.
The provisions of this subpart apply to all procurement activities.

9.402 Policy.
(a) The Government's policy is to conduct business only with responsible contractors.
(b) A contractor that has been debarred or suspended shall not receive contract awards during the debarment or suspension period.
(c) Contractors listed on the Excluded Parties List System (EPLS) or in SAM as debarred, suspended, or ineligible shall not receive contract awards.

9.403 Definitions.
Debarring official means an agency head or a designee authorized by the agency head to impose debarment.
Suspending official means an agency head or a designee authorized to impose suspension.

9.405 Effect of listing.
(a) Contractors debarred, suspended, or proposed for debarment are excluded from receiving contracts.
(b) Agencies shall not solicit offers from, award contracts to, or consent to subcontracts with these contractors unless a compelling reason exists.

9.406 Debarment.
9.406-1 General.
The debarring official may debar a contractor for:
(a) Conviction of or civil judgment for fraud, bribery, false statements;
(b) Violation of Federal antitrust statutes;
(c) Commission of embezzlement, theft, or other offense indicating a lack of business integrity;
(d) Willful failure to perform in accordance with contract terms;
(e) OFAC sanctions violations;
(f) Export control violations.

9.407 Suspension.
9.407-1 General.
The suspending official may suspend a contractor suspected of committing offenses described in 9.406.

9.5 Organizational and Consultant Conflicts of Interest

9.500 Scope of subpart.
This subpart prescribes responsibilities, general rules, and procedures for identifying, evaluating, and resolving organizational conflicts of interest.

9.6 Contractor Team Arrangements

9.601 Definition.
Contractor team arrangement means an arrangement in which two or more companies form a partnership or joint venture to act as a potential prime contractor.
